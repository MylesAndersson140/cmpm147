Experiment 03 - Alternate Worlds

Description

Dungeons are the cornerstone of rougelike gameplay whether it be above or below ground. I used dungeons as my inspiration for today and created an everchanging landscape with trees, houses, ponds, and a nice field. As you increase the seed you'll see the shape of the dungeon change, the trees, houses, ponds all change locations. Beyond that you'll notice a creeping fungal infestation inside the dungeon, noted by the red and gray tiles. Additonally, it looks like some fish may be active in the pond with all those bubbles changing!

Technical

Autotiling and Life!

Going step by step here are some of the most interesting portions of this assignment:

The changing of the walls to compliment the different dungeon sizes was thanks to a lookup array that assisted me in finding complimentary tiles with context information based on neighboring tiles. After recieving this information, I used a function that offset the current tile to create a smooth transtition from the tile I just placed. This was all possible due to assigning a 4-bit code to every tile that I then assessed before placing down the next tile to ensure smooth transitions.

Then, to create the "living" portion of the level design, I utilized noise functions to change the tiles on a whim. This is how the funal infestations spreads and how the bubbles in the pond change.
